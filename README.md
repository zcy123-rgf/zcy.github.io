## 1. Motivation & Research 
### 1.1 Project Background & Motivation
Suzhou Maple Bridge is a significant cultural heritage site with rich poetic history and Grand Canal culture. However, current on-site experiences are mostly static, text-based, and lack sufficient interaction, leading to low visitor engagement, weak immersion, and poor memorability. Traditional heritage visits cannot meet the needs of modern visitors, especially young people and international tourists. This project aims to solve these pain points by building a playful, immersive, and human-centric interactive experience using XR, generative AI, and location-based storytelling.

### 1.2 Literature & Competitor Analysis
Existing research and products show that AR and AI effectively improve heritage experiences, but most solutions focus on information display rather than playful interaction. Few designs support dual modes for tourists and local users, and many lack stable location-based interaction and gamified motivation systems. This project fills these gaps by combining guided exploration, AR effects, AI dialogue, and fragment collection.

### 1.3 User Groups & Stakeholders
The main users include international tourists, domestic visitors, local students, and residents. The project provides two modes: Quick Explore for short, fun experiences and Deep Explore for in-depth cultural learning.

---

## 2. User Needs 
### 2.1 User Journey Map
The complete user journey:
Home → Map → Story Point → AR / AI / Photo Task → Unlock Fragment → Leaderboard

### 2.2 Core User Needs
From field research and user feedback, four key needs are identified:
- Location-based exploration and navigation
- Accessible and understandable cultural interpretation
- High interactivity and strong immersion via AR and AI
- Sustainable motivation through gamification and collection

### 2.3 Field Research & Evidence
On-site observations at Maple Bridge show that traditional signboards are not attractive to young users. Visitors prefer visual, interactive, and concise content instead of long texts. International users also need multilingual support. These findings support the design of Maple Echoes.
